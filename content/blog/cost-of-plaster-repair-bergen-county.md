Here's what you can expect to pay for common plaster repair projects in Bergen County:

| Repair Type | Size/Scope | Price Range |
|-------------|------------|-------------|
| Small crack repair | Hairline cracks up to 6 inches | $75 - $150 |
| Medium crack repair | Cracks 6-24 inches or small holes | $150 - $300 |

## What Affects the Cost?

Several factors influence the final cost of your plaster repair project:

### Age and Type of Plaster
Older homes in Bergen County often feature traditional lime-based or horsehair plaster, which requires specialized knowledge and materials to repair properly. These authentic restoration techniques typically cost 20-30% more than standard gypsum plaster repairs.

### Extent of Damage
Surface cracks are relatively simple fixes, but deeper structural issues, water damage, or failing plaster keys (the backing that holds plaster to lath) require more extensive work. What looks like a small crack on the surface might reveal larger problems once we start the repair.

### Location and Accessibility
Repairs on high ceilings, stairwells, or hard-to-reach areas require additional equipment and safety measures. [Ceiling repair](/services/ceiling-repair/) work, in particular, often costs 15-25% more due to the complexity of overhead work.

### Texture Matching Requirements
If your walls have a specific texture or finish, we'll need to recreate that pattern to ensure a seamless repair. Our [texture matching](/services/texture-matching/) service ensures your repairs blend invisibly with existing surfaces.

### Integration with Other Services
Many homeowners combine plaster repair with [painting](/services/painting/) or other improvements. Bundling services often provides better overall value than tackling projects separately.

### Current Market Conditions
Like all construction services, plaster repair costs can fluctuate based on material prices and labor availability. In 2026, we're seeing stable pricing in Bergen County, making it a good time for homeowners to address needed repairs.

## Is This Covered by Insurance?

Insurance coverage for plaster repair depends on the cause of the damage:

**Typically Covered:**
- Storm damage from wind, hail, or fallen trees
- Sudden water damage from burst pipes or appliance failures
- Vandalism or break-ins that damage walls

**Usually Not Covered:**
- Normal wear and tear over time
- Gradual settling cracks in older homes
- Damage from deferred maintenance
- Cosmetic improvements or upgrades

If you're unsure about coverage, it's worth checking with your insurance company before starting repairs. We can provide detailed documentation and photos to support your claim when applicable.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does plaster repair take?
Most small to medium repairs can be completed in 1-2 days, including drying time. Larger projects might take 3-5 days. Weather and humidity can affect drying times, especially during Bergen County's humid summers.

### Can I do plaster repair myself?
While small crack repairs might be DIY-friendly, larger areas or structural issues require professional expertise. Improper repairs can lead to recurring problems and potentially cost more to fix correctly later.

### How do I know if plaster needs repair or replacement?
If more than 30% of a wall surface is damaged, or if the plaster is pulling away from the lath backing, replacement might be more cost-effective than repair. A professional assessment can help you make this determination.

### Will repairs match my existing wall texture?
Yes, professional plaster repair includes matching the existing texture and finish. This is one reason why hiring experienced contractors is important – achieving invisible repairs requires skill and the right techniques.
